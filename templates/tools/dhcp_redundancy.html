DHCP redundancy

Per-network DHCP-binding audit. A network is co-located when its two-or-more DHCP bindings all run on the same physical host — losing that host loses DHCP for the network, even though the binding count looks redundant. Surfacing them here, and in the move tool's "Other bindings" column, lets you spread bindings across hosts before they bite you.
